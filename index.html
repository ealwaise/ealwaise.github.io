About Me

Hello! I'm a mathematics Ph.D. living in Brooklyn, NY, currently working for a startup called VoiceWriter. Since I was a kid, I've loved building things like Legos, theme parks in Rollercoaster Tycoon, or houses in the Sims. My love for design led me to think about a career in software engineering. While in graduate school, I taught myself Python by building a GUI to help Starcraft players design custom maps. However, realizing I wanted to put my mathematical skills to use, I started considering other options. This led me straight to data science, where an amazing intersection between software engineering and mathematics can be found.

I love to share knowledge with others - one of my favorite aspects of graduate school was teaching. When someone asks me for help understanding something, I usually ask at least one "counter" question, both because understanding the other person's thought process is crucial to answering their question, and because learning how others think is the most interesting aspect of teaching. In my spare time, I like to play a Korean rhythm game called Pump It Up, where you step on arrows in time to music. If you want to hear more about it or talk to me for any other reason, please don't hesitate to reach out to me by email or on link LinkedIn.
